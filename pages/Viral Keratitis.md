alias:: Viral Corneal Infection
ICD-11_Code:: 1C45.1 (Viral Keratitis)
tags:: disease, eye

- ## Etiology
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Causative Agents
		- Herpes Simplex Virus
		- Herpes Zoster Virus (HZV) (if associated with V1 dermatome)
		  *[[Herpes Zoster Ophthalmicus]]*
		- Adenovirus (rare, usually mild)
		- Enterovirus (rare, severe in immunocompromised)
	- ### Risk Factors
		- Prior HSV Infection (Latent virus reactivation)
		- Immunosuppression (HIV, chemotherapy)
		- Trauma or ocular surgery (triggering viral reactivation)
		- Contact lens wear (secondary bacterial/fungal risk)
- ## Epidemiology
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Incidence
		- HSV keratitis : ~1.5 million cases globally/year; leading cause of corneal blindness in developed nations
		- HZV keratitis : 25% of [[Herpes Zoster Ophthalmicus]] cases
	- ### Prevalence
		- Higher in older adults (HZV) and young/middle aged (HSV)
		- ### Demographics
			- HSV : Bimodal (children <5yo, adults 30-50yo)
			- HZV : >50yo or immunocompromised
	- ### Geographic Distribution
		- Global; HSV-1 seroprevalence >70% worldwide
- ## Pathophysiology
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Mechanism of Disease
		- Primary infection : HSV enters corneal epithelium -> dendritic ulcers (virus replicates in epithelial cells)
		- Stromal Keratitis : Immune mediated (T-cell response to viral antigens) -> corneal scarring/neovascularization
		- Neurotrophic Keratopathy : Corneal hypoesthesia (nerve damage from recurrent HSV/HZV)
	- ### Affected Systems/Organs
		- Cornea (epithelium, stroma, endothelium)
		- Trigeminal nerve (HZV: V1 dermatome)
- ## Clinical Features
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Clinical Presentation
		- #### Symptoms
			- Pain, photophobia, blurred vision, redness, tearing
			- HSV : Recurrent episodes ("cold sore" prodrome)
			- HZV : Unilateral dermatomal rash (forehead/nose)
		- #### Signs
			- HSV
				- Dendritic ulcer (branching, flurescein-positive)
				- Geographic ulcer (advanced, amoeboid shape)
				- Stromal infiltrates (non-necrotizing or necrotizing)
			- HZV
				- Pseudodendrites (no terminal bulbs, plaque-like)
				- Disciform keratitis (stromal edema without ulceration)
	- ### Stages or Classification
		- Epithelial (active infection)
		- Stromal (immune-mediated)
		- Endothelitis (rare, HZV-associated)
- ## Diagnosis
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Diagnostic Criteria
		- Clinical(slit-lamp findings)
		- PCR/viral culture
	- ### Investigation
		- ### Establishing Diagnosis
			- Slit-lamp exam
				- Dendrites
				  *Fluorescein staining : Highlights dendritic ulcers*
				- Stromal Haze
				- Corneal sensation test
			- PCR
			  *corneal scrape/tears*
				- Gold standard for HSV/HZV
			-
		- ### Ruling Out Differential Diagnosis
			- |DD|Ruling Out|
			  |--|--|
			  |Bacterial keratitis|Purulent discharge, rapid progression|
			  |Fungal keratitis| Feathery infiltrates, trauma history |
			  |Acanthamoeba| Contact lens use, severe pain|
		- ### General Assessment
- ## Management and Treatment
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Medical Treatment
		- HSV Epithelial
			- Topical antivirals
				- Acyclovir 3% ointment
				- Ganciclovir 0.15% gel
		- HSV Stromal
			- Topical Steroids (prednisolone 1%)
			- Prophylactic oral antivirals
		- HZV
			- Oral antivirals (valacyclovir/famciclovir 7-10 days)
			- Topical steroids if stromal involvement
	- ### Surgical Options
		- Penetrating keratoplasty (corenal scarring)
		- Tarsorrhaphy (neurotrophic ulcers)
	- ### Lifestyle and Dietary Recommendations
		- Avoid triggers (stress, UV light)
		- No contact lenses during active infection
	- ### Follow-up
		- 1-2 weeks
- ## Prognosis
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Expected Course
		- #### With Treatment
			- Epithelial HSV : Heals in 1-2 weeks
			- Stromal HSV : May require months of therapy; risk of scarring
		- #### Without Treatment
			- ##### Possible Complications
				- Conjunctivitis (adenovirus)
				- Anterior uveitis (HSV, HZV)
				- Postherpetic neuralgia (HZV)
				  *common in elderly*
				- Corneal perforation, blindness (HSV stromal keratitis, *after ulceration*)
				- Episcleritis (HSV)
				- Scleritis (HSV, HZV)
			- ##### Long-Term Effects
