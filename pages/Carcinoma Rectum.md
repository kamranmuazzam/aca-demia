alias:: Ca Rectum, Rectal Carcinoma
ICD-11_Code:: 2B92.0 (Malignant neoplasm of rectum)
tags:: disease

- ## Etiology
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Causative Agents
		- No single causative agent, but genetic mutations (APC, KRAS, TP53) and epigenetic changes play a role
			- FAP gene
			- FPP coli (Familial Polyposis coli)
		- Ulcerative Colitis (pre-malignant condition)
		- Consequence of Rectal Polyp (Adenocarcinoma)
		- Radiation
	- ### Risk Factors
		- Age > 50 years (now common in young patients)
		- Familial Disease
		- Smoking
		- Obesity
		- Low fiber/high fat diet
		- Alcohol consumption
		- Sedentary Lifestyle
- ## Epidemiology
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Incidence
		- ~43k new cases/year in the US
	- ### Prevalence
		- Higher in developed countries
		- ### Demographics
			- More common in males
			- African Americans at higher risk
	- ### Geographic Distribution
		- Highest in Western countries, lower in Asia and Africa (though rising with Westernized diets)
			- 2nd after Breast Cancer
- ## Pathophysiology
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Mechanism of Disease
		- Adenoma-carcinoma sequence (from polyp to invasive cancer)
	- ### Affected Systems/Organs
		- Rectum -> may metastasize to liver, lungs, lymph nodes
- ## Clinical Features
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Clinical Presentation
		- Ulcerative
			- Per-rectal bleeding
			- Mucous Discharge
		- Cauliflowr
			- Tenesmus/morning weeping
			- A2 T circumference involve : 6 months
			- Full circumfernce involve : 12-18 months
		- Annular + Tubular
			- Alteration of bowel habit
			- Spurious diarrhoea
			- Subactue intestinal obstruction
			- Acute intestinal obstruction (Complete)
		- #### Symptoms
			- Hematochezia
			- Tenesmus
			- Alteration in bowel habits
			- Fatigue
		- #### Signs
			- DRE
				- Ulcerative
					- Shallow depressed Lesion
					- Everted Edge
				- Cauliflower
					- Elecated Hard, Irregular endoluminous mass
			- Anemia
			- Obstruction Signs (Late Stage)
	- ### Stages or Classification
		- Classification
			- Macroscopic
				- Ulcerative
				- Cauliflower
				- Annnular
				- Tubular
			- According to part of Rectum
				- Upper 1/3rd : Annular, Tubular Variety
				- Middle 1/3rd : Cauliflower
				- Lower 1/3rd : Ulcerative
		- TNM Staging (T1-T4, N0-N2, M0-M1)
		- DUKE Staging
			- Stage A
				- Disease Process/Malignancy confined to rectal wall
			- Stage B
				- Malignancy involved the para rectal tissue
			- Stage C
				- Lymph node involvement
				- Stage C1
					- Pararectal lymph node
				- Stage C2
					- The Lymph nodex involved the root of the supplying vessels
			- Stage D
				- Distant metastasis (Blood Borne)
					- Liver
					- Lung
					- Bone
					- Brain
- ## Diagnosis
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Diagnostic Criteria
		- Histopathology (biospy) confirming adenocarcinoma
	- ### Investigation
		- ### Establishing Diagnosis
			- Proctosigmoidoscopy + Biopsy -> Histopathology
		- ### Staging
			- Metastasis
				- Lung : X-Ray Chest
				- USG of abdomen (liver and para-aortic lymph node, ascites)
				- Liver Function test
					- Serum bilirubin X
					- Î³ glutamyl transferase
				- MRI of pelvis (to see extension)
				- Transrectal USG (local extension of disease)
				- CBC
			- Tumor Markers
				- CEA levels
				  *Carcinoembryogenic Antigen*
					- Non Smoker : 0-5 ng/ml
					- Smoker : 0-10 ng/ml
				- Monitoring, but no diagnostic and prognostic value
			-
		- ### Ruling Out Differential Diagnosis
			- |DD|Ruling Out|
			  |--|--|
			  |Hemorrhoids|Anoscopy, Absence of Malignancy|
			  |IBD|Colonscopy, Histopathology|
		- ### General Assessment
			- CBC
- ## Management and Treatment
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Medical Treatment
		- Neoadjuvant chemoradiation (FOLFOX, CAPOX)
	- ### Surgical Options
		- Low anterior resection (LAR)
		- Abdominoperineal Resection (APR)
		- TAMIS
	- ### Lifestyle and Dietary Recommendations
		- High-fiber diet, exercise, smoking cessation
	- ### Follow-up
		- Surveillance colonoscopy
		- CEA monitoring
		- Imaging

		- #### With Treatment

		- #### Without Treatment

				- Fistula

		- #### With Treatment
			- 5 year survivial
				- Stage I : 90%
				- Stage I-II : ~60%-70%
