- ## Management and Treatment #ent-prof-card #ent-prof-written #unanswered #[[Otitis Media]] #card
  background-color:: blue
  id:: b1c1548a-73e5-4d11-9249-1f0f1b2b101e
  deck:: #ENT
  - ### Medical Treatments
    - Antibacterial Therapy
    - Decongestant Nasal Drops
    - Oral Nasal Decongestants
      - Pseudoephedrine + Antihistamine (if combined, then nasal drops not required)
    - Analgesics and Antipyretics
    - Ear Toilet
    - Dry Local Heat
  - ### Surgical Options
    - Myringotomy
  - ### Lifestyle and Dietary Recommendations
    - Keep Ear Dry
  - ### Follow-up
    - if not improved within 10 days, change Antibiotics
    - If fluid continues then periodic check for 12 weeks, and then if Persistent effusion then Treat as [[Glue Ear]]
    - carefully followed till tympanic membrane returns to its normal appearance and conductive hearing loss disappears.
