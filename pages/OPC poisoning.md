- ## Epidemiology
  background-color:: blue

	- ### Prevalence
		- ### Demographics

	- ### Geographic Distribution
		- Endemic : Rural farming communities
		- Urban : Suicidal cases
- ## Pathophysiology
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Mechanism of Disease
		- Irreversible inhibition of acetylcholinesterase (AChE) -> Excess acetylcholine -> Overstimulation of muscarinic & nicotinic receptors
		- Aging : Irreversible bond formation after 24-48 hours
	- ### Affected Systems/Organs
		- Neuromuscular : Fasciculations, paralysis
		- Respiratory : Bronchospasm, respiratory failure
		- CNS : Seizures, coma
		- Cardiac : Bradycardia, QT prolongation
- ## Clinical Features
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Clinical Presentation
		- #### Symptoms (SLUDGE + BB)
			- Salivaton, Lacrimation, Urination, Diarrhoea, GI upset, Emesis
			- Bronchorrhea, Bronchospasm, Bradycardia
		- #### Signs
		  *Muscarinic + Nicotinic + CNS*
			- Muscarinic : Miosis, sweating, wheezing
			- Nicotinic : Muscle twitching, weakness
			- CNS : Confusion, seizures, coma
	- ### Stages or Classification
		- Acute cholinergic crisis : first 24-48 hours
		- Intermediate syndrome : 24-96 hours, respiratory failure
		- Delayed neuropathy (weeks later)
