- #definition Pneumonia is infection of lung parenchyma
- ## Etiology
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Causative Agents
		- In children
			- MC cause in neonates? GBS, E. coli, Lysteria
			- MC cause 1 mo-3 mo? C. trachomatis, RSV, parainfluenza, strep pneumoniae
			- MC cause in kids 4 mo- 5 y/o? VIRAL! (RSV) then *S. Pneumoniae*
			- MC cause in kids > 5 y/o ? *Mycoplasma*, *S. pneumoniae*
		- Bacteria
			- *Streptococcus pneumoniae* (most common)
			- *Mycoplasma pneumoniae*
			- *Legionella peumoniae*
			- *Clamydia pneumoniae*
			- *Haemophillus influenzae*
			- MC if Multiple lesions -> *Staphylococcus pneumoniae* , *Mycobacterium tuberculosis*, Fungal
			- MC in Diabetis -> *Klebsiella pneumoniae*, *Pseudomonas*
		- Virus
			- Influenza
			- Parainfluenza
			- Corona (SARS, MERS, Covid)
			- Measles
			- Herpes Simplex

- ## Clinical Features
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Clinical Presentation
		- #### Symptoms
			- Fever
			- [[Cough]]
			- Rusty Sputum
			- Haemoptysis
			- Chest Pain
		- #### Signs
			- Temp ↑
			- Respiratory Rate ↑
			- Heart Rate ↑
			- #### Respiratory System
				- Inspection
					- Respiratory Rate ↑
					- Central Cyanosis
				- Palpation
					- Chest Expansion Reduced
					- Vocal Fremitus ↑
					- Temperature ↑
				- Percussion
					- Dull over affected area
				- Auscaltation
					- Breath Sound : "Bronchial"
					- Vocal resonance : ↑ in affected area.
					- Pleural rub if pleurisy present
					- Added sound : Crepitation usually resolution phase  এ present
	- ### Stages or Classification
		- According to Site Invloved
			- Lobar Pneumonia
				- Homogenous consolidation of one or more lobe of lung
			- Bronchopneumonia
				- Patchy, alveolar consolidation associated with bronchial or bronchiolar inflammation after affecting both lobe
		- Clinically
			- Community acquired
			- Hospital acquired
			- Pneumonia in immunocompromised
			- Suppurative pneumonia

- ## Diagnosis
  background-color:: blue
	- ### Clinical Diagnosis
		- #### Classical Presentation
			- Kid comes w/ cough productive of yellow-green sputum, runny nose and T=100.8. Lung exam reveals coarse rhonchi, decreased breaths sounds and crackles in the LL and WBC = 16K
				- Next best step? CXR to confirm. Typical vs atypical
		- #### Probable Diagnosis
	- ### Laboratory Tests
		- CBC
			- eosinophilia (chlamydia pneumonia) C/S : Staccato cough
		- ESR
		- CRP
		- Serum Creatinine
		- LFT
		- [[CURB - 65 Screening]]
		- Blood Culture
		- After Blood Glucose
	- ### Imaging Studies
		- Chest XRay, PA view
			- inhomogenous opacity in --- part of chest with air bronchogram
	- ### Differential Diagnosis
		- Acute Bronchiotis
			- Kid comes w/ cough productive of yellow-green sputum, runny nose and T=100.8, Lung exam only reveals coarse rhonchii.
	- ### Confirmation of Diagnosis
		- CXR
